What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

1. Primary Goal – Lower Log P

  • Introduce polarity: add H‑bond donors/acceptors (e.g., OH, NH₂, carbonyl, sulfonamide) on the periphery of the molecule, preferably on the long C₅ alkyl chain or on one of the phenyl rings.
  • Trim hydrophobic bulk: consider shortening or branching the C₅ alkyl chain (e.g., replace CCCCC with a shorter C₃–C₄ chain or a polar ether/ester). This reduces the aliphatic contribution to log P without destroying the scaffold.
  • Replace a phenyl with a hetero‑aryl: swapping one phenyl for a pyridine, pyrimidine, or a fused heterocycle adds nitrogen/oxygen atoms, increasing polarity and often lowering log P while preserving aromatic character (good for similarity).

2. Maintain Similarity ≥ 0.4

  • Core must stay intact: the bicyclic lactam (‑C1=NC2(CCCC2)C(=O)N1‑) and the central “‑Cc1ccc‑…‑cc1” phenyl that links to the triazole‑bearing biphenyl must be retained.
  • Changes should be peripheral: modifications limited to the terminal alkyl chain or to one of the outer phenyl rings are less likely to drop similarity below 0.4.

3. Secondary Checks – Keep Drug‑Like Profile

  • Molecular weight: any added hetero‑atoms or small polar groups should keep MW well below ~550 Da (current 428 Da gives ~120 Da headroom).
  • Hydrogen‑bond balance: adding 1–2 donors/acceptors is fine; avoid >5 donors or >10 acceptors.
  • tPSA: aim for ≤ 120 Å²; each added hetero‑atom contributes ~12–20 Å², so modest additions are safe.
  • Rotatable bonds: keep ≤ 10; replacing a flexible alkyl chain with a slightly more rigid hetero‑aryl can even reduce rotatable bonds.
  • QED: target ≥ 0.5; drastic polarisation can lower QED, so balance polarity with overall drug‑likeness.
  • Lipinski violations: stay at zero; any new functional group should not introduce a violation (e.g., avoid adding a second basic amine that would raise the cLogP‑adjusted HBD count).

  • Alkyl‑chain truncation / functionalisation
