What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

3. Chemical‑Design Strategies to Achieve the LogP Drop

  • Replace the para‑fluoro substituent on the phenyl ring with a hydrogen or a small polar group (e.g., –OH, –NH₂). The fluorine contributes to lipophilicity; removing it reduces logP with minimal impact on similarity.
  • Introduce a polar substituent on the peripheral cyclopentyl ring that was previously a simple C₅ ring (e.g., convert the cyclopentyl to a hydroxy‑cyclopentyl or morpholine). This adds H‑bond capacity and polarity without dramatically increasing MW.
  • Shorten the side‑chain ether (the –CCO group) to a –CH₂OH or –CH₂NH₂. This reduces hydrophobic carbon count and adds a donor/acceptor, lowering logP.
  • Swap the N‑alkyl substituent on the fused heterocycle (currently a C5CCCC5 ring) for a pyrrolidine or azetidine ring. Smaller, more nitrogen‑rich rings increase polarity and reduce logP while preserving scaffold similarity.
  • Avoid adding large aromatic or alkyl groups (e.g., no new phenyl rings, no long alkyl chains) as they will raise logP and MW.

4. Practical “Do‑Not‑Do” List

  • Do NOT replace the central quinazoline‑like core or the piperazine scaffold – these are essential for similarity.
  • Do NOT introduce additional aromatic halogens (Cl, Br, I) – they increase logP.
  • Do NOT add more rotatable bonds (e.g., long flexible linkers) – they can degrade binding and increase entropy.
  • Do NOT increase MW beyond ~460 Da – risk of violating Lipinski and solubility limits.
