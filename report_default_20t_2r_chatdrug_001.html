What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

  • Any proposed modification must retain at least 30 % structural similarity to the starting molecule.

4. Tactical Recommendations for the Computational Chemist

  • Replace the tert‑butyl‑alcohol fragment (CC(C)(O)) with a smaller, more polar group (e.g., a hydroxy‑ethyl or morpholine moiety). This cuts ~30 Da and adds polarity.
  • Modify the terminal aromatic system (/C=C/c2ccc3ccc(Cl)cc3n2).
- Consider de‑chlorination (Cl → H) or swapping the chlorine for a fluorine or a methoxy group to reduce logP.
- Optionally truncate one phenyl ring (convert the fused bicyclic aryl to a single phenyl) while preserving the hetero‑aryl nitrogen that contributes to similarity.
  • Introduce a carbonyl or sulfonyl on the linker SCC1(CC(=O)O)CC1. For example, oxidize the thioether to a sulfone (S(=O)2) or replace the thioether with an ether (O). This adds polarity and reduces logP without breaking the core scaffold.
  • Add a basic nitrogen (e.g., piperidine or pyridine) on the side chain to increase H‑bond acceptors and potentially improve solubility.
  • Limit the number of new rotatable bonds introduced; prefer cyclic or constrained replacements (e.g., replace a flexible alkyl chain with a cyclopropyl or azetidine ring).

5. Success Metric for This Round

  • Any candidate that achieves logP ≤ 5 and retains a similarity ≥ 0.30 to the starting scaffold will be considered a clear win and should be ADVANCED at the end of the round.
  • If logP improves but remains > 6, the candidate must at least be Pareto‑non‑dominated (i.e., better logP without worsening MW, HBD/HBA, or similarity) to be considered for advancement.
