What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

1. Primary Goal – Lower logP

  • Target reduction: Aim for at least a 0.5–1.0 unit drop (≈ logP ≤ 3.9) in the first round, which would be a clear improvement without drastic structural changes.
  • How to achieve it:
- Introduce polar functionality (e.g., hydroxyl, amide, sulfonamide, morpholine) on the aromatic rings or on the aliphatic chain.
- Replace or modify the CF₃ group (highly lipophilic) with a less hydrophobic substituent (e.g., –F, –Cl, –CF₂H, or a small polar group).
- Reduce aromatic surface area modestly (e.g., replace one phenyl with a heteroaryl such as pyridine or pyrimidine) while preserving the overall scaffold.

2. Secondary Constraints – Keep the rest “in‑range”

  • Molecular weight: Stay ≤ 350 Da (avoid large appendages that would push MW far above the current 309 Da).
  • Hydrogen‑bond donors/acceptors & tPSA: Adding 1–2 H‑bond donors/acceptors is acceptable; keep tPSA below ~80 Å² to avoid solubility penalties.
  • Rotatable bonds: Ideally ≤ 8; a modest reduction (e.g., by cyclising a side‑chain) is welcome but not mandatory.
  • QED & Lipinski: Maintain QED ≥ 0.8 and zero Lipinski violations. Any design that introduces a violation (e.g., > 5 H‑bond donors) must be rejected outright.

3. Similarity Guardrail

  • Similarity ≥ 0.4 to the parent scaffold is the hard cutoff.
  • Recommended approach: Keep the central tertiary carbon attached to the amine and the ether linkage intact; modify peripheral substituents (aryl rings, CF₃, side‑chain length).
  • Avoid radical scaffold jumps (e.g., replacing the phenyl‑ether entirely with a heterocycle or deleting the aromatic ring) because they will likely fall below the similarity threshold.
