About These Notes

These research notes document patterns and insights discovered while analyzing results from 5 experiment configurations across the ChatDrug benchmark (20 lead-optimization tasks each). Each finding is derived from the aggregated metrics in experiments/analysis/metrics.json and corroborated by per-task inspection of optimization trajectories.

The five configurations compared are: default (all agents), no_critic (Critic removed), no_admet (ADMET Specialist removed), single (Medicinal Chemist + Computational Chemist only), and fixed_critic (Critic with PROCEED-by-default prompt).

The Critic Paradox

The Critic agent was designed to review proposals and reject low-quality modifications. Paradoxically, removing the Critic entirely produces the best QED improvement, while adding it in its default form yields the lowest. The fixed-critic variant (PROCEED-by-default) lands in between.

Configuration Avg ΔQED Validity Rate Avg Similarity Success Rate
Default +0.010 88.3% 0.697 90%
No Critic +0.042 85.8% 0.622 90%
Fixed Critic +0.026 — 0.709 100%
Insight: The default Critic appears to be too conservative—rejecting proposals that would have improved drug-likeness. Removing it sacrifices ~2.5 percentage points of validity (88.3% → 85.8%) but gains 4× the QED improvement. The fixed-critic variant partially recovers by defaulting to PROCEED, suggesting the Critic's value lies in flagging egregious errors rather than gatekeeping every proposal.

Hardest Molecules

Tasks 004 and 007 consistently appear among the hardest across configurations. Both start with high LogP (>5.5), high molecular weight (>550 Da), and at least 2 Lipinski violations. These molecules are already at the edge of drug-likeness, making meaningful optimization extremely difficult.

Pattern: Molecules with starting LogP > 5.5 and MW > 550 Da have a significantly higher failure rate. Both tasks require simultaneous reduction of multiple properties without breaking the core scaffold—a challenge that frequently exceeds the 2-round optimization budget.
3
SMILES Generation Weakness

In tasks 004 (no_critic config) and 006 (single config), the Medicinal Chemist agent proposed chemically sound modifications—correct reasoning about which functional groups to replace and why—but generated invalid SMILES strings that could not be parsed by RDKit.

This is a fundamental limitation of text-based molecular generation: the LLM understands medicinal chemistry concepts but struggles with the precise syntax of SMILES notation (bracket matching, ring-closure digits, stereochemistry markers).

Task Config Chemistry Reasoning SMILES Validity
chatdrug_004 no_critic Correct Invalid syntax
chatdrug_006 single Correct Invalid syntax
Implication: A dedicated SMILES validation and correction step (or a constrained decoding approach) could recover these otherwise-successful optimizations. The validity rates across all configs (85.8%–88.9%) suggest that roughly 1 in 8 generated molecules has a syntax error.
4
ADMET Preserves Similarity

The ADMET Specialist agent acts as a structural conservatism force. When present, it encourages modifications that maintain pharmacokinetic properties, which correlates with higher Tanimoto similarity to the starting molecule. Removing it drops average similarity by ~13%.

Configuration ADMET Agent Avg Similarity Δ vs Default
Default Active 0.697 —
Fixed Critic Active 0.709 +0.012
Single Absent 0.701 +0.004
No Critic Active 0.622 -0.075
No ADMET Absent 0.605 -0.092
Takeaway: Configs with the ADMET agent active average 0.697 similarity (default) vs 0.605 without it (no_admet)—a 13% drop. This is the largest single-agent effect on structural preservation, confirming that ADMET guidance acts as an implicit similarity constraint.

6
Two Rounds May Not Be Enough

In the default configuration, 17 out of 20 tasks terminated with the reason max_rounds_reached. The experiment budget was set to 2 rounds, while the config default allows up to 5. This means the vast majority of tasks were cut short before the optimization loop could converge.

Only 3 tasks terminated early with specific failure reasons (all in tasks 007 and 016, which failed outright). Every successful task used exactly 2 rounds—the maximum allowed—suggesting they could have benefited from additional iterations.

Configuration Max Rounds (Budget) Tasks Hitting Max Avg Rounds Used

No Critic 2 17 / 20 (85%) 1.9
No ADMET 2 17 / 20 (85%) 1.9
Single 2 17 / 20 (85%) 1.9
Fixed Critic 2 4 / 4 (100%) 2.0
Recommendation: Future experiments should increase the round budget to at least 5 (the config default) to determine whether additional optimization rounds yield meaningful improvement. The current 2-round cap prevents us from measuring the true convergence behavior of the multi-agent system.

Summary: No single configuration dominates across all metrics. No Critic achieves the best QED and LogP improvement but at the cost of lower validity and similarity. Single offers the highest validity among full runs. Default is the most conservative, with the highest similarity but lowest QED gain. The right configuration depends on whether the optimization goal prioritizes structural preservation or property improvement.
