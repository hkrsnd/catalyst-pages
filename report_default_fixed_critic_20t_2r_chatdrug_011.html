What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

Absorption

The modest increase in polarity (TPSA ≈ 74 Å²) and the drop in logP place the molecule comfortably within the “good oral absorption” window (logP < 5, TPSA < 90 Å²). No new H‑bond donors are introduced, so passive permeability should remain adequate.

Distribution

Lower logP reduces the risk of excessive plasma‑protein binding and improves aqueous solubility, which is favourable for achieving therapeutic exposure. No basic amine is now buried in a highly lipophilic environment, so the volume of distribution is unlikely to be extreme.

Metabolism

Morpholine is a known soft spot for oxidative metabolism (N‑dealkylation, O‑dealkylation, and possible ring‑opening). This introduces a moderate metabolic liability that could shorten half‑life relative to the cyclopentyl analogue. However, the heterocycle is tertiary and sterically shielded, so the liability is not severe. No obvious CYP‑inhibitory motifs (e.g., hetero‑aryl‑nitrogen‑oxides, quinones) are present.

Excretion

The increase in MW (≈ 475 Da) is still well below the renal clearance cutoff (~ 500 Da). The molecule will likely be eliminated via a combination of hepatic metabolism and renal excretion of metabolites; no red‑flag for poor clearance.

Toxicity

No structural alerts such as Michael acceptors, epoxides, nitro‑aromatics, anilines (the aniline is already present in the parent and tolerated), or known PAINS. Morpholine itself is not a class‑wide toxicophore; it is widely used in FDA‑approved drugs. No obvious reactive or electrophilic centres are introduced.
