What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

Concrete Tactical Guidance for the Chemists

  • Replace the CF₃ group
Rationale: CF₃ contributes ~1.5–2.0 logP units. Substituting it with a less lipophilic group (e.g., F, Cl, CF₂H, or a small polar substituent like OH or NH₂) can drop logP substantially while preserving aromatic character and similarity (> 0.4).
Suggested options:
- F (single fluorine) – minimal size change, ~0.5 logP reduction.
- Cl – slightly less lipophilic than CF₃, ~0.8 logP reduction.
- CF₂H – retains some fluorine character but adds a hydrogen bond donor potential.
  • Introduce a polar substituent on the phenyl ring attached via the ether
Rationale: Adding an ortho‑ or para‑OH, NH₂, or OCH₃ increases H‑bond capacity and tPSA, pulling logP down. Keep the substitution pattern simple to stay above the similarity threshold.
Suggested options:
- p‑OH or p‑NH₂ on the aromatic ring bearing the ether.
- meta‑OCH₃ (adds polarity with minimal steric impact).
  • Modify the ether linkage
Rationale: Replacing the aryl‑O‑aryl ether with a hydroxyl, amine, or amide introduces a donor/acceptor pair and reduces lipophilicity.
Suggested options:
- O → NH (aryl‑NH‑aryl) – adds one H‑bond donor.
- O → CONH (aryl‑carboxamide) – adds carbonyl H‑bond acceptor and donor.
  • Trim unnecessary rotatable bonds if possible
Rationale: The side‑chain CNCCC contributes three rotatable bonds. Converting the terminal amine to a secondary amide (e.g., C(=O)N) can lock one bond and add polarity without large MW penalty.
