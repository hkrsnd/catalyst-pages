What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

Concrete Tactical Recommendations for the Computational Chemist

  • Alkyl‑chain trimming / hetero‑atom insertion
Replace part or all of the CCCC chain with a –CH₂–O–CH₂– or –CH₂–NH–CH₂– fragment. This reduces hydrophobic surface and adds H‑bond acceptors/donors, directly lowering logP.
Consider removing the terminal C (the isopropyl‑type carbon attached to the aniline nitrogen) or converting it to a –CH₃ or –CF₃ (the latter would increase logP, so avoid).
  • Aromatic substitution
Swap the Cl on the fused hetero‑aryl core for a F (slightly less lipophilic) or a hydroxyl/methoxy group. A phenolic OH adds polarity without dramatically changing the core.
If the chlorine is essential for activity, keep it but explore a para‑fluoro or meta‑fluoro analogue on the same ring system (still ≥0.4 similarity).

Convert the secondary amine –NH– linking the aliphatic tail to the heteroaryl into an amide (–C(=O)NH–) or a sulfonamide (–SO₂NH–). Carbonyls are strong polarity contributors and will pull logP down.
  • Ring‑constrained analogues
Replace the flexible CCCC chain with a piperidine or morpholine ring (maintains a similar number of heavy atoms, adds a heteroatom, reduces rotatable bonds). This can cut logP while preserving the spatial relationship of the terminal amine to the heteroaryl core.
