What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

1. Primary Goal – Reduce logP

  • What to modify:
- The CF₃ substituent on the phenyl ring is a major contributor to lipophilicity. Replacing it with a less hydrophobic group (e.g., F, Cl, CF₂H, CH₃, or a small polar substituent such as OH, NH₂, CONH₂) is the most direct way to lower logP.
- Introducing hydrogen‑bond‑donating/accepting functionalities (e.g., amide, sulfonamide, morpholine) on the aromatic rings can also pull logP down while adding polarity.
- Partial de‑aromatization (e.g., converting one phenyl to a heteroaryl) can reduce hydrophobic surface area without dramatically altering the scaffold.
  • Constraints:
- Any replacement must keep the Tanimoto similarity ≥ 0.3 to the starting scaffold (the core quinazoline‑pyridine‑pyrimidine system). Small changes on the peripheral phenyl are well within this limit.
- Avoid adding large polar groups that would inflate MW > 550 Da or create a second Lipinski violation (e.g., multiple amides).
- Keep the overall HBD/HBA balance roughly unchanged; adding a single donor/acceptor is acceptable, but a cascade of them will push tPSA > 150 Å².

2. Secondary Goal – Maintain / Slightly Improve Drug‑likeness

  • Molecular weight: Any substitution should not increase MW by more than ~30 Da. Replacing CF₃ (≈69 Da) with F (≈19 Da) or CH₃ (≈15 Da) actually reduces MW, which is beneficial.
  • Rotatable bonds: Do not introduce flexible linkers (e.g., alkyl chains) that would increase rotatable bonds beyond 10.
  • QED: While not the primary metric, a modest rise (e.g., from 0.30 to > 0.35) can be achieved by balancing polarity and size; this will be a nice bonus if the logP drop is achieved without penalty.
