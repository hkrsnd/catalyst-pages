What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

3. Similarity constraint (≥ 0.4 to the starting scaffold)

  • Any modification must retain the core bicyclic hetero‑aryl system and the N‑alkyl side chain that defines the scaffold.
  • Large scaffold‑disrupting changes (e.g., ring opening, complete substitution of the aromatic core) are disallowed.
  • Substitutions on peripheral atoms (Cl → F/–OH, addition of –OH on the terminal ethyl, insertion of an ether/amine in the aliphatic chain) are typically safe for the 0.4 similarity floor.

4. Suggested design directions for this final round

  • Introduce a second hydroxyl on the terminal ethyl group (e.g., convert –CH₂CH₂– to –CH₂CH(OH)–) – adds polarity, modest MW increase (~16 Da), likely drops logP by ~0.3–0.5.

- Cl → F reduces logP modestly (~0.2) with minimal MW change.
- Cl → OH adds polarity (≈ 0.5–0.7 logP reduction) but adds ~16 Da; still within MW budget.
  • Insert an ether oxygen into the aliphatic chain (e.g., –CCCC– → –CCOCC–) – adds H‑bond acceptor, reduces hydrophobic surface, modest MW increase.
  • Swap one N‑ethyl substituent for a methyl‑hydroxyethyl (e.g., N‑(CH₂CH₂OH)CH₃) – similar to the current best but with an extra donor/acceptor.

5. Decision criteria for advancing a candidate

  • logP ≤ 3.5 (or the lowest achievable without violating other constraints).
  • Similarity ≥ 0.4 to the original SMILES.
  • No new Lipinski violations; HBD ≤ 3, HBA ≤ 6, TPSA ≤ 80 Å².
  • MW ≤ 380 Da.
  • QED ≥ 0.70 (or comparable to current best).
  • The molecule must not be Pareto‑dominated by any previously evaluated structure (i.e., it should improve logP without worsening all other metrics).
