What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

1. Primary objective – Lower logP

Target for this round: a ≥ 0.5‑unit reduction (logP ≈ 4.3 or lower).
This is a realistic first‑step improvement that still leaves room for a second round of fine‑tuning.
 How to achieve it (chemically):
- Introduce polar functionality without dramatically increasing MW or rotatable bonds (e.g., a hydroxyl, primary amine, or carbonyl).
- Replace the lipophilic chlorine on the aromatic ring with a less hydrophobic substituent (F, H, or a small polar group such as –OH).
- Shorten or cap the ethoxy ester (e.g., switch CCO‑C(=O) to CO‑C(=O) or to a simple amide) to reduce the alkoxy contribution to logP.
- Add hetero‑atoms (N, O) into the fused aromatic system if it can be done while preserving the core scaffold and similarity.

4. Decision criteria for advancement (end of round)

A proposed molecule will be ADVANCED only if all of the following are satisfied:
  • logP ≤ 4.3 (≥ 0.5‑unit improvement) and not worse on any other objective by > 10 % (e.g., MW increase ≤ 30 Da, HBD ≤ 2, HBA ≤ 5, tPSA ≤ 70 Å²).
  • Similarity ≥ 0.4 to the starting scaffold.
  • No new Lipinski violations (still 0).
  • Pareto‑non‑dominated: the molecule is not strictly worse than any previously evaluated structure on all metrics.
If after this round no candidate meets these criteria, we will re‑evaluate whether a more aggressive structural change is warranted or consider termination after the second round.
