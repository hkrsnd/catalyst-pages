What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

- Keep tPSA roughly in the 40–80 Å² range (adding a hydroxyl or amide will raise it modestly but stay within acceptable limits).

4. Recommendation (Round 1)
  • Primary objective – lower log P:
- Best is Proposal 3 (log P = 4.28).
- Second best is Proposal 2 (log P = 4.37).
  • Secondary considerations (MW, QED, polarity):
- Proposal 2 gives the largest overall improvement: it lowers log P the most among the “clean” candidates, reduces MW by 16 Da, improves QED (0.752 vs 0.705), and leaves H‑bond count and tPSA unchanged.
- Proposal 1 also reduces MW and eliminates the single rotatable bond, but its QED drops slightly and log P reduction is modest.
  • Proposal 3 achieves the greatest log P drop, but at the cost of increased polarity (extra H‑bond acceptor, +13 Å² tPSA) and a slight QED penalty. If the project tolerates a higher polar surface (e.g., to gain solubility) this could be acceptable, but it may compromise membrane permeability.
Given the current priority (minimize log P while keeping the molecule drug‑like and not inflating size or polarity), the most balanced choice is Proposal 2.

If the team is willing to explore a higher‑polarity scaffold for a larger log P gain, Proposal 3 can be kept as a backup for later SAR cycles.
