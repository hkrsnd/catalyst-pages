What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

  • Introduce Polar Functionality
- Add hydroxyl, amide, or sulfonamide groups on the peripheral phenyl ring or on the linker (e.g., replace the –OC‑CH₂‑aryl with –O‑CH₂‑CH₂‑OH or –O‑CH₂‑CONH₂).
- Consider hetero‑aryl replacements (e.g., pyridine, pyrimidine) for the outer phenyl to increase H‑bond acceptors without a large MW penalty.
  • Reduce Lipophilic Surface
- Replace the fluorophenyl substituent with a less hydrophobic heteroaryl (e.g., 2‑fluoro‑pyridine) or a methoxy‑pyridine.
- Trim one of the alkyl‑aryl linkers (e.g., shorten the –OC‑CH₂‑CH₂‑ segment to –O‑CH₂‑) to cut hydrophobic carbon count.
  • Maintain Core Scaffold
- Keep the thiazole‑pyridine core and the N‑(CH₂‑CH₂‑O‑CH₂‑CH₂‑N) morpholine‑like side chain, as they contribute positively to QED and H‑bonding.
- Any modification should preserve at least 40 % Tanimoto similarity to the original SMILES; avoid wholesale scaffold changes (e.g., swapping the thiazole for a completely different heterocycle).

- Each added heteroatom typically adds ~15–20 Da and ~10–15 Å² to tPSA. Limit the net MW increase to ≤ 30 Da and tPSA increase to ≤ 15 Å² to stay within the desired windows.
  • Synthetic Feasibility
- Prefer late‑stage functional group interconversions (e.g., O‑alkyl → O‑hydroxy, aryl‑F → aryl‑NH₂) that are synthetically tractable from the current lead.
