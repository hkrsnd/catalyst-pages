What do these metrics mean?
MW (Da)Molecular Weight (MW) — total mass of the molecule in Daltons. Drugs typically need MW < 500 Da for good oral absorption (Lipinski's Rule).
LogPLogP — a measure of how fat-soluble (lipophilic) a molecule is. Higher = more greasy. Ideally ≤ 5 for oral drugs. The optimization goal here is to minimize LogP.
HBDHydrogen Bond Donors (HBD) — groups like OH or NH that can donate hydrogen bonds. Fewer donors generally means better membrane permeability. Lipinski limit: ≤ 5.
HBAHydrogen Bond Acceptors (HBA) — atoms like O or N that can accept hydrogen bonds. Lipinski limit: ≤ 10.
tPSATopological Polar Surface Area (tPSA) — the surface area of polar atoms (N, O, S and attached H). Measured in Å². Low tPSA (< 140) favors oral absorption.
Rot. BondsRotatable Bonds — single bonds that allow free rotation, making the molecule flexible. Fewer (≤ 10) is better for oral bioavailability.
QEDQuantitative Estimate of Drug-likeness (QED) — a score from 0 to 1 combining multiple drug-likeness properties. Higher = more drug-like. Most approved drugs score > 0.3.
Lipinski Viol.Lipinski Violations — how many of Lipinski's 'Rule of Five' criteria the molecule fails (MW, LogP, HBD, HBA). Zero violations is ideal; ≤ 1 is acceptable.

1. Primary objective – lower logP

Target: bring logP down by at least 0.5 units (i.e., ≤ 4.2) in this round, while keeping everything else acceptable. Why 0.5? A reduction of 0.5 logP typically translates into a noticeable improvement in solubility and ADME risk without demanding radical scaffold changes that would jeopardise similarity.

2. Constraints that must be respected

ConstraintRationale
Similarity ≥ 0.40 to the parentGuarantees we stay on‑track with the original chemotype and avoid “drift”.
MW ≤ 450 Da (preferably ≤ 420 Da)Prevents the molecule from becoming too heavy, which would offset any logP gain with poorer permeability.
HBD ≤ 2 and HBA ≤ 5The parent already has a very clean H‑bond profile; adding many donors/acceptors can raise polarity but also increase metabolic liability.
tPSA ≤ 80 Å² (ideally ≤ 60 Å²)Keeps the molecule in the “drug‑like” window for passive diffusion.

QED ≥ 0.70Maintains overall drug‑likeness; the current 0.789 gives us a comfortable buffer.
No new Lipinski violationsMust stay within the rule‑of‑five envelope.

4. “Do‑not‑do” list for this round

Remove the phenyl ring entirely – would drop similarity below the 0.4 threshold. Add bulky polar groups (e.g., long PEG chains) – would raise MW > 500 Da and rotatable bonds dramatically. Introduce charged groups (e.g., carboxylates, quaternary amines) – would cause a huge logP drop but also break similarity and likely create a new ionisation state, complicating ADME. Add multiple H‑bond donors – could push HBD > 2 and increase polarity beyond the desired window.
